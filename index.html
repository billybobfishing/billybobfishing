04 — Heritage

Three generations.
One stubborn waterline.

Billy Bob Sr. started welding johnboats in a Bayou La Batre shed in 1961. His grandson welds destroyers. The bench in between never changed — same calipers, same coffee can of bolts, same refusal to ship a hull that won't bring everyone home.

We don't see a contradiction between a 4,200-tonne frigate and a $14 frog. Both have to survive bad water and worse luck. Both get the same signature on the QA card.
